Letter I never wanted to send

You found this because you're special.
This isn’t just a birthday wish — it’s a quiet thank you for being the reason behind some of my brightest smiles and softest songs.

But some things are left unsaid for too long, and maybe they start to hurt in silence. So, for once, I’m letting it all out — because my heart needs this closure, even if yours never asked for it.

I never thought I’d reach a point where expressing how I feel would hurt this much.

I gave everything I could to keep us alive — I begged, stayed patient, chased, and fought silently just to hold on to something that once made me feel the happiest. But somewhere along the way, I lost myself… and what hurts the most is that you didn’t even notice.

I know you were going through your own struggles. You weren’t in the best mental space, and you said you didn’t have time. And honestly, I was happy whenever you did call, even if it was just for a moment. But deep down, I felt disrespected. I was showing up with effort and care, but it felt like I was being taken for granted. No one is ever too busy to send a two-second reply. And I found myself asking for the bare minimum — just basic human decency — and even that felt like too much for you.

I get being busy... but not forgetting. Not silence. Not making someone feel invisible. You used to care. You used to talk to me like I meant something. Now, I’m just... here. Waiting for replies that never come. Wondering what I did wrong.

I never asked for grand gestures. I didn’t even want constant attention. But going days, even weeks, without a single message? Not even a ten-second check-in? That silence said more than any words ever could.

I still keep my phone next to me while sleeping hoping that your name would pop up, still wait for your good morning texts/calls but they never came.

And then there was that day in Oia — your birthday. That moment broke something in me. I was right there beside you, trying to make the day special, trying to make you feel loved. But still, I felt so alone. You asked me why I was quiet — and I couldn’t say it then — but it was because I had never felt so invisible next to someone I loved so much. I had imagined us being close that day, laughing, sharing that beautiful place together. But you didn’t even look at me properly. I know you were excited — you’d been waiting to visit that place for so long — but I was there too… and it felt like you forgot that.

When you went back home, I stayed here hoping, praying, wishing that everything would be okay for your family. And every time you called — scared, anxious, or lonely — I was there. No matter where I was, I picked up. I supported you in every way I could. But looking back now… I think I was wrong. It felt like once things started getting better on your end, you slowly began drifting away. And in the end… what happened? You moved on. While I was still hoping and praying for your family’s peace, you were already letting go of us.

I never had bad intentions. I never wished anything wrong for you — not even for a second. But I honestly didn’t think it would end like this. Not with you. Not like this.

Lately, I’ve been taking therapy. I needed it — because holding in all of this pain was starting to break me from the inside and giving me panic attacks. It got so intense that I couldn’t sleep at night, appetite became worse and the worst thing , I got rude to everyone around me. And slowly, I’m trying to heal, trying to understand, trying to breathe again. But some wounds… they take time.

Take care of yourself. And yes, don’t overthink too much. I genuinely want you to stay happy. Just… next time, please learn to respect someone who truly cares about you and worries for you. And please, stay aware of people around you in Bangalore — especially the men.

And yes... I still love you. Maybe not in the same way, not with the same hope — but with the part of me that once believed in us. This is the last time I’ll say it… and I mean it.

If I knew that the airport hug will be our last hug, I’d have hugged you for little longer. Thank you for all the good memories. I’ll cherish them forever.

Goodbye — and I hope life treats you kindly.

No matter where life takes us, remember this:
Someone out there smiles a little wider when they hear your name. 💖

Forever a Swiftie by your side,
— Rishab (Taylor's Version)
