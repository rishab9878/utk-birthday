Letter I never wanted to send

You found this because you're special.
This isn’t just a birthday wish — it’s a quiet thank you for being the reason behind some of my brightest smiles and softest songs.

Hey,

I never thought I’d reach a point where expressing how I feel would hurt this much.

I gave everything I could to keep us alive — I begged, stayed patient, chased, and fought silently just to hold on to something that once made me feel the happiest. But somewhere along the way, I lost myself… and what hurts the most is that you didn’t even notice.

I know you had your own battles. You weren’t in the best mental space. You said you didn’t have time — and I understood that. I never expected daily calls or grand gestures. But what I did expect was basic respect. A two-second reply. A message to say “I’m okay.” That’s all.

Instead, I felt like I was constantly begging for the bare minimum. And when silence becomes louder than words, it’s hard not to feel invisible. You used to care. You used to talk to me like I mattered. Now I was just... there. Waiting for replies that rarely came. Wondering what I did wrong.

I still kept my phone beside me at night, hoping your name would pop up. I waited for those little things. But they never came.

And then Oia — your birthday. That broke something in me. I was right there, trying to make it special for you. But you didn’t even really look at me that day. You asked me why I was so quiet — and I couldn’t say it then — but it was because I had never felt so alone beside someone I loved so deeply. I was invisible even when I was right next to you

When you went back home, I prayed for your family. I was there whenever you needed me — anxious, scared, or low — and I showed up every single time. But once things started getting better, I felt you slowly drift away. You moved on. And I was still here — hurting, praying, hoping — for all of it to somehow feel real again.

I never had bad intentions. I never wished anything wrong for you — not even for a second. But I honestly didn’t think it would end like this. Not with you. Not like this.

In the past two months, I’ve been to therapy because the all these became so much — the panic attacks, the sleepless nights, the way I began snapping at people who didn’t deserve it. I didn’t recognise myself anymore. And I’m still trying. Still healing. Still figuring it out.

Please take care of yourself. And yes, don’t overthink too much. I genuinely want you to stay happy and safe. Just… next time, please learn to respect someone who truly cares about you and worries for you. And please, stay aware of people around you in Bangalore — especially the men.

And yes… I still love you. Not with the same hope, but I’ll be waiting — if someday in the future you want to come back, I’m ready to accept you as you are. Because some parts of love… don’t fade. They just learn to stay quiet.

If I knew that airport hug would be our last… I would’ve held on for a little longer.

Goodbye — and I hope life treats you kindly.

No matter where life takes us, remember this:
Someone out there smiles a little wider when they hear your name. 💖

Forever a Swiftie by your side,
